We add <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>69</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">69</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">6</span><span class="mord">9</span></span></span></span></span> to it and finish our tour.</li>
</ul>
<p>Clearly these operations take time proportional to the height of the tree. And since we’ve constructed a full binary search tree, the height and thus the runtime, is <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>lg</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{O}(\lg n)</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 1em; vertical-align: -0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right: 0.02778em;">O</span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mop">l<span style="margin-right: 0.01389em;">g</span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.166667em;"></span><span class="mord mathdefault">n</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span></span>.</p>
<p>It remains to look closely at the vertex indices of a full binary search tree to further optimize this process. We naturally look at the binary representations of the indices.</p>

<p>Here’s a cool observation (try proving it maybe): <em>when we move from a child <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi>u</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.43056em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord mathdefault">u</span></span></span></span></span> to its parent, the last 1 (from left) in the binary representation of <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi>u</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.43056em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord mathdefault">u</span></span></span></span></span> moves one step to the left (and possibly merges with another 1 if there is already one)</em>. Let’s call this operation <em>a shift</em>. So a shift transforms a vertex to its parent. Equipped with this, we can revisit our algorithms to retrieve sums and perform updates.</p>
<p>In order to compute <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>a</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo>+</mo><mo>⋯</mo><mo>+</mo><msub><mi>a</mi><mn>11</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a_1+\cdots+a_{11}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.73333em; vertical-align: -0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathdefault">a</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.301108em;"><span class="" style="top: -2.55em; margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height: 2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.15em;"><span class=""></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.222222em;"></span><span class="mbin">+</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.222222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.66666em; vertical-align: -0.08333em;"></span><span class="minner">⋯</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.222222em;"></span><span class="mbin">+</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.222222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.58056em; vertical-align: -0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathdefault">a</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.301108em;"><span class="" style="top: -2.55em; margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height: 2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1</span><span class="mord mtight">1</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.15em;"><span class=""></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> we need to collect sums at vertices on the way from vertex <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>11</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">11</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mord">1</span></span></span></span></span> to root that have indices at most <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>11</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">11</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mord">1</span></span></span></span></span>. But notice that a shift decreases the original value if there’s a merge happening, and increases the value otherwise. For example: <code>1010</code> transforms into <code>1100</code> after a shift which is bigger. But <code>1011</code> transforms into <code>1010</code> after a shift which is smaller - this is because the last 1 merges with the one on its left and thus, essentially vanishes. We can utilize this observation to <em>skip</em> all the shifts without merges and move to the next merge: <code>1101 -&gt; 1110 -&gt; 1100</code> simplifies into <code>1101 -&gt; 1100</code>. This is essentially <em>removing</em> the last 1 bit: <code>1101 - 0001 = 1100</code>. So our algorithm looks like this: start at vertex <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>11</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">11</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mord">1</span></span></span></span></span>, collect sum, remove last 1, repeat (till root).</p>
<p>In order to add <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>69</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">69</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: -0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathdefault">a</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.301108em;"><span class="" style="top: -2.55em; margin-left: 0em; margin-right: 0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height: 2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight">5</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height: 0.15em;"><span class=""></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> we need to update all vertices on the way from vertex <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">5</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">5</span></span></span></span></span> to root that have indices at least <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">5</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">5</span></span></span></span></span>. We recall the observation that a shift decreases the original number if there’s a merge happening, and increases otherwise. So we need to <em>skip</em> all the shifts with merges and move to the next shift without merge: <code>1011 -&gt; 1010 -&gt; 1100</code> simplifies into <code>1011 -&gt; 1100</code>. This is essentially <em>adding to</em> the last 1 bit: <code>1011 + 0001 = 1100</code>. So our algorithm looks like this: start at vertex <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">5</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.64444em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord">5</span></span></span></span></span>, add value, add to the last 1, repeat (till root).</p>
<p>And finally, let’s look at a trick to isolate the last 1 bit in a number <code>x</code> - it is given by <code>x &amp; -x</code>. Here’s why: let’s write the number as <code>x = a10..0</code> where the <code>1</code> is the last one in <code>x</code>. Then <code>-x</code> is found by inverting all bits in <code>x</code> and adding <code>1</code> (two’s complement). We get <code>-x = b01..1 + 1 = b10..0</code> where <code>b</code> is found by inverting all bits of <code>a</code>. So <code>x &amp; -x = a10..0 &amp; b10..0 = 0..010..0</code> with only the last <code>1</code> bit of <code>x</code> retained at its position.</p>
<p>Combined with all this, we get the following procedures that take up <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{O}(n)</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 1em; vertical-align: -0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right: 0.02778em;">O</span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathdefault">n</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span></span> memory in whole and <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>lg</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{O}(\lg n)</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 1em; vertical-align: -0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right: 0.02778em;">O</span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mop">l<span style="margin-right: 0.01389em;">g</span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.166667em;"></span><span class="mord mathdefault">n</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span></span> time per query.</p>

  while (p &lt;= n) a[p] += v, p += p &amp; -p;  
}

int sum (int p) {
  int ret = 0;
  while (p) ret += a[p], p -= p &amp; -p;
  return ret;
